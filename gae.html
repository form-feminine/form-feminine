Gae Aulenti

Gae Aulenti (1927–2012) was an Italian architect and designer, celebrated for her museum designs and adaptive reuse projects. She merged architecture and interior design seamlessly, creating transformative spaces.

Her most notable works include the Musée d'Orsay renovation in Paris and the Palazzo Grassi in Venice. Aulenti combined historical sensitivity with bold interventions, bridging past and present.

She emphasized functional beauty and attention to detail, earning a reputation as a meticulous and visionary designer.

Aulenti’s career inspired women in architecture by demonstrating that elegance, technical expertise, and cultural awareness can coexist in powerful architectural works.

Her legacy continues to influence architects and designers, emphasizing that thoughtful design respects history while innovating for modern use.

"Architecture is a language, and I want to be a poet." – Gae Aulenti
