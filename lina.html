Lina Bo Bardi

Lina Bo Bardi (1914–1992) was an Italian-born Brazilian modernist architect whose work blended social engagement with inventive design. She believed architecture should serve communities and cultural life.

Her masterpieces include the São Paulo Museum of Art (MASP) and SESC Pompeia. Her designs often incorporated raw materials and bold geometric forms, creating functional yet inspiring spaces.

Bo Bardi emphasized participatory architecture, integrating public life into her projects and redefining modernism through local context and human experience.

She championed simplicity and accessibility in architecture, proving that elegance does not require extravagance, and that social purpose enhances design impact.

Her legacy continues to inspire architects globally to blend modernist aesthetics with cultural and societal consciousness.

"Architecture is not about space but about time." – Lina Bo Bardi
