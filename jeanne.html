Jeanne Gang

Jeanne Gang (born 1964) is an American architect and founder of Studio Gang, recognized for her innovative use of materials and environmentally conscious designs. She merges technical skill with social awareness.

Her notable works include Aqua Tower in Chicago and the Arcus Center for Social Justice Leadership. Gang's architecture emphasizes context, sustainability, and the relationship between people and urban spaces.

She has been lauded for blending structural ingenuity with aesthetic beauty, creating iconic forms while addressing climate and societal needs.

Gang is celebrated for advancing the role of women in architecture, showing that thoughtful, responsive design can redefine urban life.

Through her work, she inspires architects to innovate responsibly, merging artistry with functional solutions for communities.

"I see architecture as a way to enhance human experience, not just to build." – Jeanne Gang
